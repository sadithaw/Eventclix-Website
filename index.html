Welcome to EVENTS.lk

Welcome to our website, where you can find information about events and traditions in Sri Lanka. Sri Lanka is a beautiful country located off the southern coast of India, known for its stunning beaches, ancient temples, and delicious cuisine. It has a rich culture and history, with a variety of traditions and events that are celebrated throughout the year. On our website, you can find information about traditional festivals and celebrations, such as the Sinhala and Tamil New Year, Navam Perahera (a Buddhist festival featuring elephant pageants), and the Esala Perahera (a Buddhist festival featuring fire-dancers and drummers). You can also find information about modern events and happenings in Sri Lanka, including music festivals, sporting events, and cultural performances.Whether you are a visitor looking to experience the unique culture of Sri Lanka, or a local looking to stay up-to-date on events in your community, our website is a great resource.In addition to the traditional festivals and events mentioned above, Sri Lanka has many other cultural and religious celebrations throughout the year. Some examples include: Vesak: This Buddhist holiday commemorates the birth, enlightenment, and death of the Buddha. It is marked by the construction of pandals (temporary structures) decorated with lanterns and candles, and the donation of food and other offerings to the monks. Poson Poya: This Buddhist holiday marks the arrival of Buddhism in Sri Lanka and is celebrated with processions and the offering of alms to monks. Kataragama Festival: This Hindu festival takes place at the Kataragama temple in the south of the country and is a celebration of the god Skanda. It features processions, dancing, and the offering of flowers and other offerings to the deity. Christmas: Sri Lanka has a significant Christian population, and Christmas is celebrated by both Catholics and Protestants. It is marked by the decoration of trees, the singing of carols, and the exchange of gifts. Diwali: This Hindu festival of lights is celebrated in Sri Lanka with the lighting of lamps and candles, the exchange of sweets and gifts, and the performance of puja (worship). These are just a few examples of the many festivals and celebrations that take place in Sri Lanka. We hope this information is helpful and that you will have the opportunity to experience some of these cultural events during your visit to Sri Lanka.

Srilankan Heritage Culture & Entertainment

Sri Lanka has a vibrant and exciting nightlife and entertainment scene, with a wide range of options for tourists looking to experience the local culture and have a good time. One popular option is to visit one of the country's many clubs and bars, which offer live music, DJ sets, and other entertainment. Colombo, the capital city, has a


particularly active nightlife scene, with a range of bars, clubs, and pubs catering to different tastes and interests. Another option is to experience traditional Sri Lankan entertainment, such as dance and music performances. These can often be found at cultural festivals and events, or at dedicated performance venues. Sri Lanka has a rich tradition of dance, with a number of different styles including Kandyan dance, which is a form of classical dance that originated in the central region of the country. If


you're looking for more relaxed entertainment, there are also a number of cinemas and theaters in Sri Lanka showing both local and international films and performances. In addition to these options, tourists can also enjoy a range of activities and experiences that are unique to Sri Lanka, such as visiting a local tea plantation, trying traditional Sri Lankan cuisine, or visiting one of the country's many beautiful beaches. Overall, Sri Lanka offers a wide range of nightlife and entertainment options for tourists, with something for everyone to enjoy.
