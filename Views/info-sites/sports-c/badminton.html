badmintion

Badminton is a popular sport in Sri Lanka, with many people playing the game at various levels, from amateur to professional. The sport is played on a rectangular court, with players using rackets to hit a shuttlecock back and forth over a high net. The objective of the game is to hit the shuttlecock in such a way that the opposing player is unable to return it, and to score points by landing the shuttlecock within the boundaries of the court.

Badminton is played by both men and women in Sri Lanka, and the sport is popular among people of all ages. The Sri Lanka Badminton Association is the national governing body for the sport in the country, and it is responsible for organizing and promoting badminton at all levels. There are many badminton clubs and facilities throughout Sri Lanka, where people can play the game and participate in organized tournaments and competitions. In addition to amateur play, there is also a professional badminton circuit in Sri Lanka, with many talented players competing at the national and international level.

History

Badminton has a long history in Sri Lanka, with the game having been introduced to the country in the early 20th century. It is believed that the first badminton club in Sri Lanka was established in Colombo in 1922, and the sport quickly gained popularity among people of all ages and skill levels. In the decades that followed, badminton in Sri Lanka continued to grow and develop, with many people playing the game at amateur and professional levels.

The Sri Lanka Badminton Association, the national governing body for the sport in the country, was established in 1952, and it has played a key role in promoting and organizing badminton at all levels in Sri Lanka. Over the years, the association has organized many tournaments and competitions, both at the national and international level, and has helped to nurture the development of many talented badminton players. Today, badminton remains a popular sport in Sri Lanka, with many people playing the game and participating in organized tournaments and competitions at all levels.

It was not until the 1992 Games in Barcelona that it was officially included on the Olympic programme, with men’s and women’s singles and doubles events. The mixed doubles event made its debut in 1996 at the Atlanta Olympic Games. Since then, the number of events has remained unchanged.
