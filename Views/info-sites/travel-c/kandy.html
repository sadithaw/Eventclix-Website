Kandy

Kandy is the second largest city in Sri Lanka and is located in the Central Province. It is known for its rich history and culture, and is a popular tourist destination in the country.

The city is home to the Temple of the Tooth, a sacred Buddhist site that houses a tooth relic of the Buddha. Kandy is also known for its beautiful lake, which is surrounded by a number of temples, restaurants, and other attractions.

The city is located in the midst of hills and is known for its pleasant climate. It is a great place to visit if you are interested in learning about Sri Lanka's culture and history.

History

Kandy is a city located in the central province of Sri Lanka. It is the second largest city in the country and is considered the cultural capital of Sri Lanka. The city has a long and rich history dating back to the 14th century.

According to legend, Kandy was founded by King Vikramabahu III of Gampola, who named the city Senkadagalapura. It later came under the control of the Kingdom of Kandy, which was a powerful kingdom in the central highlands of the island. The Kingdom of Kandy was known for its cultural and religious achievements and played a major role in the history of Sri Lanka.

During the colonial period, Kandy was the capital of the Kingdom of Kandy, which was a protectorate of the British Empire. The city played a significant role in the struggle for independence and was the site of the Kandy Convention, which marked the end of British rule in the country.

Today, Kandy is a major tourist destination in Sri Lanka known for its historical sites, beautiful landscapes, and cultural attractions. It is home to the Temple of the Tooth, which is a major Buddhist shrine, and the Kandy Esala Perahera, which is a colorful and elaborate festival held annually in the city.
