Sigiriya

Sigiriya is an ancient rock fortress located in the central Matale District of Sri Lanka. It is situated in the midst of the jungle and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The fortress is believed to have been built by King Kashyapa in the 5th century. It consists of a series of galleries, corridors, and stairways that lead to the top of the rock, which offers panoramic views of the surrounding area.

The most famous feature of Sigiriya is the Mirror Wall, which is a highly polished wall that was used by the king's palace guards to see their reflections. The wall is adorned with graffiti, which are poems and drawings that were left by visitors to the site.

Sigiriya is a popular tourist destination and attracts thousands of visitors every year.
