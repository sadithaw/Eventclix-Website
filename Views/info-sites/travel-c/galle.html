Galle

Galle is a city located on the southwest coast of Sri Lanka. It is the capital of the Southern Province and is known for its historic Dutch fort, which was built in the 16th century. The fort is a UNESCO World Heritage site and is a popular tourist attraction in Galle.

The city itself is a mix of old and new, with a blend of Dutch colonial architecture and modern buildings. Galle is also home to several museums, including the National Maritime Museum and the Galle National Museum. In addition to its cultural attractions, Galle is known for its beautiful beaches and is a popular destination for beachgoers.

The city is also home to a number of restaurants and cafes serving local and international cuisine.

History

Galle has a long and varied history. The city was founded by Arab traders in the 9th century and was later conquered by the Portuguese in the 16th century.

The Dutch arrived in the 17th century and built the fort that is now a UNESCO World Heritage site. The British took control of the city in the 19th century and ruled until Sri Lanka gained independence in 1948.

Today, Galle is a major city in Sri Lanka and is known for its historic fort and cultural attractions. It is a popular destination for tourists and is home to a diverse population made up of Sinhalese, Tamil, and Muslim communities.
