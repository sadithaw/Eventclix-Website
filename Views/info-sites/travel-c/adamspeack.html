Adam's Peak

Adam's Peak, also known as Sri Pada or "Sacred Footprint," is a mountain located in central Sri Lanka. It is a popular destination for tourists and pilgrims due to its significance in Buddhism, Hinduism, Islam, and Christianity. The mountain is named after the footprint-shaped mark on its summit, which is believed to be the footprint of the Buddha, the Hindu deity Shiva, or the biblical Adam, depending on one's faith tradition.

Adam's Peak is located in the central highlands of Sri Lanka and stands at an elevation of 2,243 meters (7,359 feet). It is a popular hiking destination and is known for its beautiful views of the surrounding landscape. The hike to the summit typically takes about 6-7 hours and is typically done at night, as the views from the top are best at sunrise.

In addition to its religious significance, Adam's Peak is also an important ecological site and is home to a number of rare plant and animal species. The mountain is surrounded by a number of forests, including the Sinharaja Forest Reserve, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

History

Adam's Peak, whose history dates back to the pre-Christian era, is special to all religions and expressed in different ways if you ask the locals about the history of this sacred mountain, hence relying on whom you are asking. Buddhists believe that it was the residence of their lord.

While Hindus hold that according to their folktale it is in Adam's Peak that Ravana, King of Asura, hid Seetha, the queen of Lord Rama after abducting her from India.

Muslims have it that after the forbidden fruit incident from Heaven, Adam directly descended on Adam's Peak. Sun-worshippers are said to have made first offerings here in 140 BC with King Dutugemunu who had given staples to monks at Sri Pada; the karma of which incident is said to have healed him at the deathbed. According to the chronicle of the ancient history of Sri Lanka, the story of Sri Pada has something to do with Queen Varija, daughter-in-law of King Kashyapa.

At various points in history, the holy destination had to face setbacks and remain away from public attention.
