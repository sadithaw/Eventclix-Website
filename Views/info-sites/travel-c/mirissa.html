Mirissa

Mirissa is a small town located on the south coast of Sri Lanka in the Matara District. It is known for its beautiful beaches, whale watching, and laid-back atmosphere. The main beach in Mirissa is long and sandy, with palm trees lining the shore. The water is warm and clear, making it a popular spot for swimming and snorkeling.

There are also several smaller beaches located nearby, such as Coconut Tree Hill and Secret Beach. In addition to the beach, Mirissa is home to a number of restaurants and cafes serving local and international cuisine.

It is a popular destination for tourists, both local and international, and is a great place to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Sri Lanka.

History

Mirissa is the largest fishing port on the south coast and is known for its tuna, mullet, snapper, and butterfish.
